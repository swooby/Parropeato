M. Parropeato

Listens to what you say through speech recognition, then reads back what it thinks it heard — in a cheerful robotic voice. Speak normally. Speak in gibberish. The results are equally entertaining.

View on GitHub

Plain silly, on purpose

Say a thing. Hear the thing. Smile at the thing.

Ropeato is a novelty app for phones and watches. It does not try to be a productivity system, a life coach, or a cloud-powered oracle. It captures what you say using speech recognition and reads back what it heard in its own little robot voice — which can differ wildly from what you actually said.

The vibe

Low fuss, quick laughs, very repeatable.

Built for the kind of five-second bit that gets funnier when it comes back slightly garbled from the phone in your pocket or the watch on your wrist.

How it goes

Three steps, no grand ceremony.

Tap

Open M. Parropeato on your Android phone or Wear OS watch.

Speak

Say the important phrase, the questionable phrase, or just “banana.”

Repeat

Hear what the speech recognizer decided you said. Spot-on or gloriously wrong — both are fun.

Privacy

A toy app should stay uncomplicated.

Ropeato does not run a developer-controlled server for storing microphone audio, speech transcripts, or text-to-speech content. The app relies on the speech services available on your device.
